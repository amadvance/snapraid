What is SnapRAID?

SnapRAID is a powerful backup and redundancy program designed for disk arrays. It stores parity information (a special type of mathematical backup) that allows you to fully recover all your data even if you experience up to six simultaneous disk failures.

It is primarily intended for home media centers and large storage arrays containing files that don't change frequently (like movies, music, and photo archives). It offers the high-end protection of a professional server but with the simplicity and flexibility of a home computer.

Why Choose SnapRAID?

SnapRAID provides a unique approach to data protection that prioritizes safety, flexibility, and energy efficiency. It is designed to sit on top of your files as a safety net, ensuring your data is protected without requiring complex changes to your hardware or operating system.

The "Safety Net" Philosophy

SnapRAID acts as a transparent layer of security that protects your data without ever taking it hostage. It treats your array as a collection of individual assets, providing a safety net that is both powerful and precise.

  • Disaster-Proof Recovery

    The primary goal is total data reconstruction. Should a hard drive fail, SnapRAID uses its parity data to rebuild the entire contents of the lost disk onto a new one. Because it can handle up to six simultaneous failures, it provides a level of resilience that scales with your needs, ensuring that a hardware crash doesn't mean a data catastrophe.

  • Defense Against the Invisible

    Physical disk failure isn't the only threat; "bit-rot" or silent errors can corrupt files without warning. SnapRAID proactively defends your archive by hashing every file. It doesn't just wait for a disk to die; it constantly verifies that your data is exactly as you left it, identifying and repairing invisible corruption before it can spread.

Maximum Freedom, Minimum Risk

SnapRAID is designed for the "real world" of home hardware. Most users don't buy ten identical 20TB drives at once.

  • Grow as you go

    You can mix a 4TB drive with a 12TB drive and add a new one whenever you find a sale.

  • No Reformatting

    You can start protecting disks that are already full of your movies and photos without moving them.

  • Zero Lock-in

    Your data is never trapped. If you decide to stop using SnapRAID, your disks remain standard drives that can be read by any computer.

Feature Spotlight

Resilient Recovery

Full Recovery

Total data reconstruction

If your hard disks die, SnapRAID can fully recover all the data from the failed drives, up to the number of parity disks you have assigned (protecting you from up to 6 simultaneous failures).

Silent Corruption Protection

Total data integrity

All your data is hashed to ensure integrity and prevent "bit-rot." If a file suffers from silent corruption, SnapRAID detects it and repairs it using your parity information.

Surgical Recovery

Undelete and restore

If you accidentally delete files on a disk, recovery is possible. SnapRAID can reconstruct missing data and even undelete specific folders without affecting the rest of the array.

Fault Isolation

Confined data loss

When the number of failed disks exceeds the parity count, data loss is confined only to the affected disks. Data on all other healthy disks remains fully accessible and untouched.

Hardware Freedom

Maximum Flexibility

Mix, match, and grow

Disks can have different sizes and different brands. You can add new disks to the array at any time as your storage needs grow, without the need for complex migrations.

Eco-Friendly Efficiency

Power-saving by design

To access a file, only a single disk needs to spin. This reduces noise, saves power, and extends the lifespan of your hardware compared to traditional RAID systems that spin every disk for every read.

Data Sovereignty

Non-Locking Integration

Access your data, your way

You can utilize disks already filled with files without the need to reformat them. Access your files as usual, even without the program running. SnapRAID doesn't lock in your data; you can stop using it anytime without moving a single byte.

Open Source & Free

Transparent, Community-Driven, and Cost-Free
